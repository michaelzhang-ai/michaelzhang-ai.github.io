TrafficPredict: Trajectory Prediction for Heterogeneous Traffic-Agents

AAAI 2019 (Oral, acceptance rate 16%)

Yuexin Ma, Xinge Zhu, Sibo Zhang, Ruigang Yang, Wenping Wang, Dinesh Manocha

Abstract

To safely and efficiently navigate in complex urban traffic, autonomous vehicles must make responsible predictions in relation to surrounding traffic-agents (vehicles, bicycles, pedestrians, etc.). A challenging and critical task is to explore the movement patterns of different traffic-agents and predict their future trajectories accurately to help the autonomous vehicle make reasonable navigation decision. To solve this problem, we propose a long short-term memory-based (LSTM-based) realtime traffic prediction algorithm, TrafficPredict. Our approach uses an instance layer to learn instances' movements and interactions and has a category layer to learn the similarities of instances belonging to the same type to refine the prediction.
